The agency of an individual is
dependent on proximity, a measure
of closeness between experience
and interpretation. Greater proximity in
time or space allows greater potential
for alteration and control of
an experience. The applications for
this project focus on the inherent
power structures in viewing.


Iterative explorations of proximity
enforce the idea that perceptions
of experience are susceptible to
conscious and unconscious change
depending upon the distance of
the viewer. This susceptibility gives
the viewer power to reinterpret and
revise the experience of another into
their own ideological system.
